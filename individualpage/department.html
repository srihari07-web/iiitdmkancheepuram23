Role of Design and Manufacturing in Computer Science and Engineering

IIITDM Kancheepuram’s Computer Science and Engineering is modelled on the ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) recommendations and is the first of its kind engineering program offered in India. This program is aimed at producing engineers equipped with skills required for efficient hardware-software interaction. In addition to courses offered by the conventional Computer Science , this novel program offers courses such as Embedded Systems, Human-Computer Interaction, Signals and Systems, Product Design and Prototyping etc., that equip the students with both computing and electronics engineering skills that are very much required for the successful creation of products requiring hardware - software interactions. Our graduates would find wide scope in VLSI, Embedded Systems and Electronics Product Manufacturing related industries in addition to application development avenues and higher studies that are open to conventional Computer Science engineers. Role of Design and Manufacturing in Electronics Engineering

The electronics industry is the world’s largest and fastest-growing industry and is increasingly finding applications in all sectors of the economy. The electronics sector is one of the essential pillars of both the “Make in India” and “Digital India” programs of the Government of India. Besides the economic imperative, focus on electronics hardware manufacturing up to the integrated circuit or chip level is required due to the growing security concerns. As per the National Policy on Electronics (NPE) 2019, India has a $400 billion turnover target in the electronics industry by 2025. The Indian electronics sector has set a stage in bringing a mark for the country on the global map to become an “Electronics Design and Manufacturing Hub”.

Technologies such as IoT, Augmented Reality/Virtual Reality, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning all need well-fetched electrical/electronic components for the hardware. In order to achieve sustainable economic growth, it is crucial to manufacture Indian-designed electronics products with innovative technologies. Electronics Products Design and Manufacturing has a strong potential for India to be the top-noted choice in the semiconductor industry globally.

With respect to the talent pool, there is still a significant gap in the interest and skill set of graduates as the awareness and knowledge assimilated by them at the University level is not up to the level that is expected at an industry standard. In order to fulfil this knowledge gap effectively, IIITDM Electronic and Communication Engineering has been structured focusing on the design and manufacturing aspects of the electronics chip design industry. So, we have the unique opportunity to design and manufacture electronics with the help of the country’s current and future technical graduates. More about the department, programmes and courses offered can be found at the department website

Design-centric Mechanical Engineering

Human beings and machines are interacting in new and unique ways in 21st century. If you want to build them, you have got to design them. Our department focuses on Design-centric Mechanical Engineering which emphasize on System Thinking, Intelligent Design, Professional Ethics/Sustainability, Usability Engineering, Sociology in Design and Product System as well as services. The manufacturing industries in India are re-inventing itself to embrace digital technologies (Industry 4.0), and supply chain gaps to create a self-reliant India. Some of the unique practices implemented inline with this enhancement include a series of design and manufacturing courses to identify and translate ideas to proof of concepts. Our department focuses on creating design-centric thinkers, solution providers, entrepreneurs, and leaders. Design-centric education is fundamentally about creating a new learning environment that nurtures curiosity and calls for a more unified approach to development of mind-body-morality, aspects that are fundamental to New Education Policy (NEP 2020). The department research area also includes robotics, biomechanics, automobile, aerospace, energy and electric vehicles, wearable devices, among others. More about the department, programmes and courses offered can be found at the department website.
