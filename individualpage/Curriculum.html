Design Centric Engineering @ both UG and PG levels

The current industrial scene not only demands technical proficiency, but necessary skills in need identification, ideation, conceptualizing and prototyping, and converting them to viable products. With the current industrial standards and consumer awareness, it is necessary to design products that are easy to manufacture, efficient and become part of users themselves.

To facilitate such requirements, design has been one of the cornerstones of the institute, in line with its vision of ‘Need - Design – Innovate – Simulate – Create’. The students have at their disposal a variety of design-based engineering courses that helps bridge the gap between industry and academia and provides opportunities for increased collaboration. The plethora of interdisciplinary courses promote flow of thought across different streams, assisting students in acquiring holistic knowledge and thereby encouraging them to become successful entrepreneurs.

Conceptualization, Visualization and Realization Practices

To facilitate such requirements, design has been one of the cornerstones of the institute, in line with its vision of ‘Need - Design – Innovate – Simulate – Create’. The students have at their disposal a variety of design-based engineering courses that helps bridge the gap between industry and academia and provides opportunities for increased collaboration. The plethora of interdisciplinary courses promote flow of thought across different streams, assisting students in acquiring holistic knowledge and thereby encouraging them to become successful entrepreneurs.

Students practice creative techniques such as morphological chart, TRIZ, Biomimicry, SCAMPER etc. The methods are practically dealt with reference to concept of real-time use. Tools for concept screening and selection such as Pugh chart and benchmark matrix are taught. Students brings aesthetic, modular and integrated architecture, universality, ergonomics etc in to their conceptual design. The design, which is initially communicated in the form of pencil sketches are developed as models using clay, plastic, sheet metal, wood, thermo-col models, 3D printed models

Product Design and Prototyping – A Stepping stone to innovation

Apart from final year projects, the students at both UG and PG level explore product design and prototyping. Based on the knowledge acquired in the design courses, they evolve designs and convert into workable models. As usual, the design usually begins with a survey and followed by a visual prototype. Upon technical analysis and review comments of the expert committee, a green signal is given to exhibit the proof of concept. The products are interdisciplinary and thus the team members are inter-department. The students are free to use rapid prototyping or traditional model making of material of suitable choice. This approach helps the students to understand the importance of interdisciplinary nature of present and futuristic product design. The students practice customer interview and House of quality in the Need stage; creative techniques such as Morphological charts, TRIZ, Bio-mimicry etc in the conceptualization phase; solid modeling software and analysis package in the analysis phase; and finally use 3D printing, prototype making in the product realization phase. A systematic approach of product design makes the students to bring aspects of engineering and technical features, commercial importance, safety features, eco-friendliness, ergonomics, aesthetics etc into the real time and need of the hour product.
